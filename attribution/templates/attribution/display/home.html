Chinese Buddhist Canonical Attributions database

The Chinese Buddhist Canonical Attributions database project is the brainchild of Michael Radich <Michael.Radich@vuw.ac.nz> and Jamie Norrish <jamie@artefact.org.nz>.

The aim of the database is to record information about ascriptions of Chinese Buddhist texts.

Key terms:

assertion
a statement about the ascription of a text, or a summary record of such a statement included in CBC@.
source
the source of an “assertion”, including traditional texts (e.g. catalogues such as Zhisheng 智昇, Kaiyuan Shijiao lu 開元釋教 錄 T2154) and works of modern scholarship (e.g. Hayashiya Tomojirō 林屋友次郎, Kyōroku kenkyū 経録研究).

The database includes all texts in the Taishō 大正 and Xuzangjing 続蔵経 collections. Further texts can be added freely. The database also includes basic data about ascriptions automatically extracted from the CBETA xml versions of those texts. It also includes “assertions” about ascriptions for a range of other texts, from a range of “sources”.

You can search for assertions about texts that interest you using the tabs at the top of the screen by keyword, persons involved in the production of the text, date (including a range of dates), or the “source” of the assertion.

There is no intention that the database itself ever be used as a direct source of information about texts or “assertions”. Rather, it should be used like an annotated index: it points users to potentially useful scholarly sources. Users should then engage directly with those sources themselves, rather than relying exclusively upon CBC@. This frees contributors from onerous responsibility for excessive accuracy; only thus can such a user-contributor database be expected to reach and maintain relatively full coverage. Put differently: The database is a venue for friendly sharing of informal notes on sources—not, itself, an authoritative source.

CBC@ is a user-contributor database, and its success depends upon contributions from qualified users. At the same time, it is a scholarly resource, and content is moderated to maintain accuracy and relevance. If you are interested in becoming a contributor to the database, please email Michael Radich <Michael.Radich@vuw.ac.nz>.
